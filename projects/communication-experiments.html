Communication Research

Communication Experiments

Overview

Testing narrative, layout and tone for polished storytelling. This project explores how different communication approaches can influence perception, engagement, and understanding of complex concepts.

We examine the intersection of content strategy, design, and psychology to develop communication frameworks that resonate with specific audiences.

Process

The research involved testing multiple narrative structures, visual hierarchies, and tonal approaches across different mediums and audiences. We documented what resonated and why.

Through iterative experimentation, we developed principles for creating communications that are both aesthetically refined and strategically effective.

Learnings

A fundamental insight is that the best communication disappears—the audience focuses on the message, not the medium. Yet the medium profoundly shapes how the message is received and understood.

Consistency in tone and voice builds trust, while strategic variation keeps communication fresh and engaging. The balance between these two is where excellence lives.

Next Steps

Future work will develop a comprehensive communication style guide based on these experiments, applicable to multiple contexts and platforms.

We will also explore how emerging mediums and formats require adaptation of communication principles while maintaining core values of clarity and elegance.
